# competitive/ — who else your customers look at

**This is Zone 4 of your Business Brain.** It's what Pocket Agent reads before it drafts anything positioning-related — a sales page, a comparison table, a rebuttal to "how are you different from X?", or a battlecard for the person on the phone.

The rule: real competitors. Not aspirational competitors ("we're like Y but for Z"). Real names your customers actually mention on discovery calls.

## What goes in here

| File | What it captures |
| :--- | :--- |
| `competitor-intel.md` | The scan. One paragraph per real competitor. Their strength, their weakness, how you beat them. |
| `deep-dive-example.md` | One competitor with the deep read — pricing, sales motion, product surface, hiring pattern, why they win, why they lose, why you win when they lose. |
| `positioning.md` | The one-line why-us. Plus positioning notes for each segment. |

## The threat rating rule

Every competitor entry carries an honest threat rating: **VERY LOW / LOW / MODERATE / HIGH / VERY HIGH**. Don't inflate. If a competitor is VERY LOW threat, say VERY LOW — that's useful signal for Pocket Agent when it drafts, because it tells the agent whether to mention them at all in a comparison.

## The "how they win when they win" rule

Every competitor entry names the specific scenario where the competitor is the right choice. This is honesty, not weakness. If a prospect fits the competitor's ICP better than yours, your agent should say so — that's the operator posture that wins referrals from the buyers you send away.

## What NOT to put here

- **Trash talk.** No "they're a scam / clueless / dying." Even if true, it reads as insecurity. State facts. Let the facts do the work.
- **Guessed pricing.** If you don't know their price, don't invent one. Say "public pricing unclear" and move on.
- **Screenshots of their site.** Not because of copyright — because screenshots go stale fast and the brain shouldn't rot on their release schedule.
- **Names of employees.** You're competing with businesses, not people. Except in the rare case a specific hire signals a specific move (a CTO hire = infrastructure play; a Head of Sales hire = enterprise motion). Then note the role, not the person's whole LinkedIn.

## How to fill this out

The two example files ship pre-filled with a composite competitive scan for the same operator business used across `products/` and `customers/`. Read the shape. Write yours.

## The refresh cadence

Refresh this zone quarterly, or when a competitor makes a real move (launch, price change, funding round, hiring pattern). Anything more frequent and you're spending brain cycles on their moves, not yours.
