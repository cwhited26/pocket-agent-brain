## Example synthesized source (delete when you fill your own)

**File name:** `example-book-chapter-3.md`

**What chapter 3 teaches:** How to build a value stack that makes the offer feel too rich to refuse. The move is enumeration + specific dollar values + a total that dwarfs the ask.

**Patterns to lift:**

1. **Enumerate every deliverable as a line item.** Not "onboarding call included" — "60-minute one-on-one onboarding call ($497 value)." Reach for this when writing any high-ticket sales page.
2. **Anchor the total against the ask.** "$4,997 in value for $497." Reach for this after the value stack is enumerated.
3. **Bonuses cluster at the bottom of the stack.** Real bonuses, not fake ones. Reach for this when the offer needs one more push to feel worth it.

**Anti-patterns:**

- The word "unlock." Killed in `voice-spec.md` §3.
- Fake scarcity ("only 3 spots left" when there are 30). If the scarcity isn't real, don't fake it.

The 30-day punch list where I stay on the hook for the bugs. And a working example of every pattern from the class, in your brain repo, so your agent has a reference the moment you close this tab. Fifteen hundred dollars.

That's the shape. Real numbers, real deliverables, real dollar price, no "unlock."
