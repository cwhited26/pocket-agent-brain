# voice/influences/ — the operators you model

This is Layer 3 of your voice infrastructure. Your voice is yours (Layer 1, `voice-spec.md`). Your *structural moves* — how you stack a value proposition, how you order a sales page, how you anchor a price, how you write an OTO — you borrow from the operators who taught them. That's what lives here.

**The composition rule.** Voice from Layer 1 + structural patterns from Layer 3. Never one without the other. When you draft a sales page: pull the structure from an influence (order, sections, moves), then run every sentence through §10 of `voice-spec.md` to make sure it sounds like you, not like them.

**The naming rule.** Use the *technique* in plain English. Don't co-opt the branded framework label. "Hook Formula," "Dream 100," "Value Ladder," "Grand Slam Offer" stay out of your copy — they're markers of someone else's brand. The move you learned from them is fair game.

## What goes in an influence folder

Each operator lives at `voice/influences/<slug>/`. Inside:

- `README.md` — one paragraph on who they are, what you use them for, and what you don't.
- One file per synthesized source — a newsletter issue, a book chapter, a podcast episode. Named for the source. Each file: what the source teaches, the specific patterns to lift, and a "when to reach for this" trigger.
- An `_AWAITING_SOURCE_MATERIAL.md` placeholder is fine if you haven't synthesized yet.

## Current influences

The default template ships one influence folder as an example — `example-influence/`. Copy the shape, fill it with operators who taught you the moves you actually use.

Common starting influences for operator-built businesses: Russell Brunson (funnel mechanics, order-form bumps, OTOs), Alex Hormozi (offer construction, anchor pricing, guarantees), Daniel Priestley (positioning, key-person-of-influence framing), Ann Handley (long-form nonfiction cadence), Dan Kennedy (direct-response ad copy).

## What's *not* an influence

- A person whose vibe you like but whose techniques you can't name. Vibes don't ship.
- A framework you read about but never used. Only what you've actually applied to your own copy.
- A brand you follow. Influences are operators, not brands.
