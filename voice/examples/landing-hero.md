## Why the on-brand version works

### Headline: "Run an AI agency from your pocket."

- **Six words.** Compressed to the point where the reader has to read it.
- **Outcome state.** Not "Learn the AI Brain System" (mechanism) or "Master AI Agency Building" (process). The outcome — running an agency, from anywhere — is the headline.
- **No abstraction.** "From your pocket" is concrete. The reader can picture it.
- **Operator-first.** Not "build an AI agency" (you build it once). "Run" (you run it daily). The verb assumes ongoing operation, not aspirational planning.

### Subheadline: "Get the AI brain that makes it possible — the system I use to run real software businesses from anywhere, with any agent."

- **Mechanism + proof of use.** "The AI brain that makes it possible" (mechanism). "The system I use to run real software businesses" (proof — Chase actually uses this).
- **First-person credibility.** "I use" — not "our customers use" / "users have reported". The seller is using the product on their own businesses.
- **Specificity buried in honesty.** "Real software businesses from anywhere, with any agent." Three concrete dimensions: real (not toy), software (not hypothetical), from anywhere (location-independent), any agent (not vendor-locked).
- **No "next-generation" / "cutting-edge" / "empower" / "transform" / "scale".** Every word is plain English.

### Anti-guru block: "Tired of $47 AI courses that teach you how to ask Claude better questions? So am I."

- **Names the bad version of the product upfront.** "$47 AI courses" — specific, not "other AI courses". The reader has bought one. So has Chase.
- **"So am I."** Three words. Aligns the seller with the buyer's frustration.
- **"This isn't a course. It isn't a prompt pack."** Names what it's not before naming what it is. Reduces the reader's "is this the thing I already bought?" cognitive load.
- **"The actual system — the AI brain — that I run my own businesses on."** Receipts in the description. The system is in use, not a theoretical framework.
- **"Platform-agnostic. Model-agnostic."** Two-word commitments. No hedge.
- **"If the agent landscape shifts tomorrow, your AI brain still works."** Names the buyer's actual fear (model churn / vendor lock-in) and answers it.

### What the off-brand version gets wrong

- **"Transform Your Business with AI-Powered Automation"** — three buzzwords, zero concrete outcome.
- **"Discover the next-generation platform"** — "discover" is browse-tone; "next-generation" is meaningless; "platform" is vague.
- **"Empowers entrepreneurs to leverage cutting-edge artificial intelligence"** — "empowers", "leverage", "cutting-edge" are all banned.
- **"Achieve unprecedented growth"** — unfalsifiable; says nothing.
- **"In today's competitive landscape"** — pure padding.

The off-brand version could be the homepage of any of 10,000 AI startups. The on-brand version is unmistakably Chase's. That's the test.
