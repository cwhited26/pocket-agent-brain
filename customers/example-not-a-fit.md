# The buyer who is NOT your customer

**Type:** Composite anti-persona. Blurred pattern of the buyer we should not sell to, based on real prospects who ate time and paid nothing.
**Status:** Standing filter. If a lead matches this profile, disqualify at intake.

---

## Who they are

The corporate side hustle. A W-2 employee at a mid-size company with an idea "for the space." They want a "co-founder who codes." They have a business plan. They have a deck. They have no customers, no revenue, no operators involved yet.

They pattern-match to an operator because they use operator vocabulary, but the vocabulary is second-hand — picked up from a podcast, not from actually running a P&L.

## Why they look promising at intake

- They can articulate a problem clearly.
- They know the right buzzwords.
- They will book a discovery call inside 24 hours.
- They will show up on time.
- They will say "yes, absolutely" to every question about budget.

## Why they close nothing

- They don't have money to spend right now — the "budget" is future revenue from a business that doesn't exist.
- They want equity, not invoices. When the ask surfaces, the deal dies.
- They will spend six weeks debating scope and never sign.
- They will ghost after the third round of revisions on the free proposal we shouldn't have written.

## The tell (catches them at intake)

Two questions catch 90% of these:

1. **"What's your current revenue in this line?"** If the answer isn't a number, it's zero. If they explain the answer, it's zero.
2. **"What's the biggest problem you're solving *this week* — not this quarter?"** A real operator has a this-week answer. A dreamer answers with quarter-scale abstractions.

If both fail: don't book the discovery call. Send a polite "sounds like you're pre-revenue — we work best with businesses doing $500k+ in active revenue. When you're there, get back in touch."

## The related trap: the "consulting partnership"

A subset of this same anti-persona shows up as a well-known consultant / coach who wants to "partner" — you build the tool for equity in their venture. Same trap, different wrapper. Same answer: no. If they want the tool, they pay for the tool. Equity is not currency for us.

## Historical patterns (delete when you write your own)

- **Case A:** 3 rounds of discovery, a full spec written free, a "co-founder" pitch on call 4, zero dollars, ~14 hours lost.
- **Case B:** A famous coach's "let's white-label your system." No P&L attached to the ask, no operating business behind it. Politely declined; would have been ~30 hours to first invoice.
- **Case C:** A friend of a customer. A friend. The awkward-decline conversation cost half an hour and one small favor. Worth it.

## The one exception

A pre-revenue buyer who has the money in the bank (real capital, W-2 income, family money, whatever) AND is willing to pay cash NOW, no equity, no discount — that's actually a customer, they just look like this profile at first glance. The rule for exception: cash first, work second. If they hesitate on the invoice, they're back in the anti-persona bucket.
