# Owner-operated storm restoration contractors, $1-5M

**Composite. Blurred. Modelled on the real primary segment behind an operator-run consulting practice.**

## Who they are

- 5-30 people. Almost always trade family or ex-trade themselves. Not MBAs.
- Doing $1-5M in annual revenue, insurance + retail mix, mostly roofing + siding + gutter.
- Between 3 and 15 years in business — past the "am I really doing this" phase, before the "we need a real ops layer" reckoning.
- Owner runs sales AND ops in Year 1-4, hires an office manager somewhere in Year 3-5, hires a real ops person maybe never.
- Mostly regional. One office, one market, one storm history to know cold.
- Phone-first. Owner runs the day from a truck.

## How they find you

- Warm intro from another contractor in a private group chat / WhatsApp. **60% of intake.**
- Loom video shared in a public roofing group (Facebook, Skool, private Discords). **~25%.**
- Cold outbound (rare, we don't push it). **~10%.**
- Content — a specific piece of writing about how a real contractor's CRM works. **~5%.**

## What they buy

- A custom CRM built to their exact workflow. $2,500 - $6,000 one-time, delivered in 6-10 weeks with a 30-day punch list.
- Optional: hosted operating stack (Vercel + Supabase on their own accounts, we set it up).
- Optional: a marketing website if their current one is embarrassing.

## What they don't buy

- SaaS licenses. They're burned on SaaS. They want to own the code.
- Enterprise-tier "workflow platforms." Won't touch them.
- Recurring managed services. They'll take hourly punch-list work.
- Anything that requires a login-heavy learning curve for the crew.

## What they're afraid of when they contact you

Real quotes from real intake calls (paraphrased, names removed):

- "I've paid three developers, none of them shipped anything."
- "I need this working before the next storm season or forget it."
- "My reps aren't going to log in to another thing."
- "I don't want to be your test project."

## The one line that converts them

**"You'll own the code, we'll ship in weeks not months, and I still have skin in the game after the launch."**

Every word of this earns its place. "Own the code" — because they've been burned by SaaS. "Ship in weeks not months" — because they need something before storm season. "Skin in the game after the launch" — because the last three developers went silent the day the invoice cleared.

## Where they hang out

- Private group chats (WhatsApp, GroupMe) — not addressable except via warm intro.
- Facebook groups for specific insurance carriers + shingle brands.
- Two or three regional trade shows a year.
- One podcast that a lot of them listen to.
- Not on Twitter. Rarely on LinkedIn.

## What they read

- Podcasts on 2x during drive time.
- Trade magazines (physical) in the office.
- The occasional operator newsletter (you'd be shocked which ones actually get opened).

## The conversion trigger

They convert when they realize *you* are one of them, not a vendor. The tell they've caught it: they start using your first name in the second reply, and the vocabulary switches from "your product" to "the thing you're building for me." Track this moment in the intake log — that's the point where the deal is real.
