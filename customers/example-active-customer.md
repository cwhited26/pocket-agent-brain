## Who they are

Owner-operated contractor, one office, ~14 people including crew. Storm restoration and retail residential. In a mid-sized metro. 4 years in business, third-generation trade family. Runs the business from a truck as much as an office. Not a laptop guy — anything you want him to use lives on his phone.

## How they found us

Warm intro through another contractor in a private WhatsApp group. Not paid. Not from a landing page. He watched a Loom, then a second Loom, then paid.

## What they bought

A custom CRM built for how *he* runs a claim. One-time $6k. Hosting is his (Vercel + Supabase, we set it up, keys are on his card).

## What they didn't buy (yet)

- Public-facing marketing site. His current site is fine; his leads don't come from search.
- Company Cam replacement in the CRM. He named Company Cam twice on the discovery call — he's happy with it.
- SumoQuote. He owns SumoQuote already and doesn't want a re-implementation.

## What he's afraid of

- Reps marking steps complete without the actual work being done. This is the #1 driver of every workflow decision — every "Mark Complete" button gets a validator.
- His trusted rep (one person) walking. The whole system is designed so a second rep could pick up a job at any stage without asking him.
- Storm season with the desk understaffed. That's why the calendar has three overlay views.

## Language he uses (lift this)

- "Claim" not "job" for insurance work.
- "Retail" for cash / homeowner-paid work.
- "Mark it complete" not "advance the workflow."
- "Insurance company" not "carrier" (careful — he'll correct you).
- "Adjuster confirmation" is a specific stage, not a synonym for "confirmation."
- "Front of the house" for the street-facing side of a property. Never "facade."
- "Kick it back" when a step needs a bypass approval.

## Language to *never* use with him

- "Leverage," "synergy," "moving forward" — corporate deck noise. He'll bounce.
- "Streamline," "unlock" — SaaS marketing register. Off-brand for him.
- "Take a look when you have a second." Tell him what to do and by when.

## How he pays

Stripe payment link, one-time. Terms are net-zero (paid before build starts) with a 30-day punch list included. Hosting billed monthly on his own Vercel + Supabase.

## Contact preferences

Notes for outreach: don't ask him to "spread the word." Ask him if he has anyone in mind for a specific offering — he'll usually name one.
